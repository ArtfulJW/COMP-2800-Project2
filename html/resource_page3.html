What's the difference between a Grab-and-Go Bag and a Home Kit?

The Home Kit is for emergencies like earthquakes, storms, or accidents where you will stay at home for the duration of the situation. However, the Grab-and-Go Bag is a smaller portable version of the Home kit, prepared for disasters that require you to evacuate quickly. Applicable situations for the Grab-and-Go bag include floods, landslides, wildfires, and tsunamis.

Grab-and-Go Bag

  • Sleeping Bag or Blanket
  • One set of all-season clothing
  • Bottled Water
  • Snacks (Stock enough for 12 hours)
  • Whistle
  • Flashlight and Batteries
  • (or hand-crank light)
  • Radio and Batteries
  • (or hand-crank radio)
  • Extra batteries, phone charger, and battery bank
  • Small first-aid kit and personal medication
  • Photocopies of emergency plans, identification, insurance papers, and other important documents
  • Cash in small bills
  • Pen and Notepad
  • Sentimental or comforting items

Home Kit

  • Water (four litres per person per day)
  • Non-perishable food (dried, canned), or emergency food rations
  • Manual can opener
  • Spices and sweeteners (salt, pepper, honey, sugar)
  • Durable cups, bowls, plates, and cutlery
  • Tissues, toilet paper
  • Personal hygiene supplies (soap, toothpaste, diapers)
  • Garbage bags and wet wipes
  • Dust masks
  • Seasonal clothing, sturdy footwear
  • Blankets
  • Personal comforts (coffee, tea, teddy bear)
  • Phone charger or battery bank
  • Cash in small bills
