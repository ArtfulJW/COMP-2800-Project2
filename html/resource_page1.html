Types of disasters

To begin preparing for disasters, you must first determine what type of disaster you are expecting to prepare for. The preparations and procedures that prepare you for surviving different hazards will vary, so it is important that you prepare accordingly. In the case of Earthquakes and Tsunamis, PreparedBC recommends that you prepare a Home Kit and Grab-and-Go Bag.
Reference

Earthquakes and Tsunamis

Earthquakes in British Columbia are actually very common, it's just that most of the time they're too weak to deal any sort of real damage. However, earthquakes strong enough to cause considerable damage come once per decade. In the case of big earthquakes, tsunamis usually follow after the earthquake.

Earthquake Procedures

The Government of British Columbia's PreparedBC details the proper steps that you need to take in the event of an earthquake. More specifically, what to do before (preparation) and during the earthquake.
Reference

Drop, Cover and Hold on

  1. As soon as you feel the ground shake, drop to your hands and knees. If you're inside - don't run outdoors or to other rooms.
  2. Cover your head and neck with your arm and take shelter under a sturdy piece of furniture. If there is no shelter nearby, crawl to the nearest interior corner or wall while continuing to protect your head and neck
  3. Hold on to your shelter, covering your head and neck until the shaking stops.
